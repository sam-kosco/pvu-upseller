## Brightwork

**What it is:** Brightwork is the process of polishing an aircraft's bare metal surfaces —
including leading edges, wing tips, nacelles, and stabilizers — to remove scratches,
pitting, hazing, and oxidation, restoring a mirror-like shine.

**Why it matters:** Aircraft brightwork is typically made from delicate aluminum alloys
that are exposed to oxygen, moisture, salt air, acid rain, and UV radiation. These elements
cause oxidation, which chemically breaks down the aluminum and leads to pitting, etching,
and permanent surface damage if left unaddressed. Regular polishing prevents corrosion
from progressing and maintains the structural integrity and appearance of metal surfaces.

**Levels of correction:**
- Light: well-maintained metal with minor hazing; addressed with fine polish grades
- Medium: standard oxidation and light scratching
- Medium Plus: heavier oxidation requiring multiple compound passes
- Heavy restoration: deep pitting or corrosion requiring rotary and aggressive compounds

**Products used:** Nuvite NuShine II system (F9 through S grades), Granitize XMP system
(6-stage color-coded), Real Clean 3-Step system. Product selection is matched to the
severity of the metal condition observed.

**Result:** A polished, uniform, mirror-like finish that resists future oxidation and
protects the metal from further degradation.

## Xylon (also spelled Xzilon)

**What it is:** Xylon (Xzilon 3 Aircraft Exterior Protector) is a corrosion-inhibiting
protective coating applied to bare metal surfaces after brightwork polishing. It is best
understood as an add-on service to brightwork rather than a standalone offering.

**Why it matters:** After brightwork restores the metal to a polished state, Xylon seals
and protects that finish for the long term. It is the finest corrosion inhibitor available
for aviation brightwork. Benefits include:
- Resists salt water spray and UV radiation
- Withstands heat up to 450 degrees
- Reduces ongoing maintenance costs by extending the interval between polishing cycles
- Postpones parts replacement by slowing corrosion-driven wear
- Increases laminar airflow over leading edges, which can result in measurable fuel savings

**Important distinction:** Xylon bonds to bare metal surfaces and is specifically designed
for brightwork (leading edges, nacelles, etc.) — it is not a paint coating.

## Ceramic Coating (Paint Correction + Ceramic Coating)

**What it is:** A two-step process combining paint correction (multi-step mechanical
buffing to restore the paint surface) followed by application of a professional-grade
ceramic coating that chemically bonds to the paint, forming a durable protective layer.

**Why it matters:** Aircraft paint is constantly exposed to UV radiation, environmental
pollution, exhaust, hydraulic fluids, bird strikes, and atmospheric abrasion. Over time
this causes oxidation, micro-scratching, swirl marks, and paint porosity — all of which
increase drag and make the aircraft harder to clean. Ceramic coating addresses both the
current condition and long-term protection.

**Paint correction:** The preparation phase is critical and what distinguishes Foxtrot's
approach. We perform a full multi-step buffing process on all painted surfaces — including
commonly missed areas like the horizontal stabilizer and crown. This removes surface
imperfections and chemically prepares the paint to bond properly with the coating. Without
proper paint correction, coatings fail prematurely.

**The ceramic coating itself:**
- Bonds chemically to paint, forming a layer denser than clear coat
- Highly resistant to gasoline, chemicals, UV radiation, and environmental contaminants
- Cannot be removed by chemicals — requires abrasion, making it genuinely long-lasting
- Incredibly hydrophobic, adding a self-cleaning effect that significantly reduces cleaning time
- Enhances gloss, depth, and color clarity
- Extends time between paint cycles, reducing long-term ownership costs
- Can improve aerodynamic performance by reducing surface porosity and drag
- Comes with a full 3-year warranty regardless of flight hours
- Foxtrot is a certified installer for this product

**Best for:** Aircraft with new or recently refreshed paint looking to protect that
investment, or older paint needing restoration and long-term protection.

## Permagard Coating

**What it is:** Permagard is an industry-standard aircraft paint protection system that
provides a high level of gloss enhancement and surface protection. It requires a yearly
booster treatment every 10–16 months to maintain its warranty and full protective
effectiveness.

**Why it matters:** Permagard protects the aircraft's painted surfaces from UV exposure,
oxidation, and environmental contamination while enhancing gloss. It is widely recognized
in the aviation industry and provides strong protection at a lower price point than ceramic
coating.

**Key distinction from ceramic:** Unlike ceramic coating, Permagard requires a periodic
booster treatment to maintain its warranty. The booster is significantly less expensive
than the original application. Ceramic coating, by contrast, bonds permanently to the
paint and requires no scheduled reapplication. For owners who want premium protection
without recurring treatments, ceramic is the superior long-term investment.

**Foxtrot's process:** The aircraft is fully cleaned, decontaminated, and paint-corrected
as needed before Permagard is applied. All applications are inspected under proper lighting
(flashlight verification) to ensure even coverage and eliminate swirl marks or high spots.
Permagard is the industry standard, the warrenty lasts for a year before reapplication is recommended.

## Polymer Coating

**What it is:** A polymer sealant that provides a temporary protective layer over aircraft
paint. Polymer sealants are easier to apply than other coatings and provide a noticeable
gloss enhancement.

**Important context:** Polymer sealants are not true ceramic coatings. They can be
stripped by fuel, chemicals, or harsh environmental exposure, making them a shorter-term
solution. Some products marketed as "ceramic" contain ceramic particles but behave as
sealants — if a product can be removed chemically, it is a sealant, not a true ceramic
coating. Polymer coatings are significantly less expensive than ceramic or Permagard and
are suitable for owners seeking a cost-effective near-term option. They are not recommended
for aircraft as a long-term solution.

**Best for:** Owners wanting paint protection and gloss enhancement at a lower price
point, or as a bridge treatment between major paint events. Typically lasts 3-6 months depending 
on hangar time and flight hours

## Interior Detail

**What it is:** A comprehensive deep cleaning of the entire aircraft interior, covering
all cabin surfaces, seating, leather, carpets, galley, lavatory, cockpit, headliners,
sidewalls, windows, and compartments.

**Why it matters:** Private aircraft interiors accumulate contaminants, body oils, food
residue, and fine particulate matter in ways that are not always visible. Regular
thorough cleaning preserves the condition of leather, upholstery, wood trim, and soft
goods — all of which degrade significantly faster without proper care. A clean interior
also reflects directly on the owner's standards and the perceived value of the aircraft.

**What's included:**
- Full vacuuming of all carpeted areas, seat tracks, crevices, aft cargo, flight deck
- Leather cleaning and conditioning on all seats, sidewalls, and headliners using
  professional-grade Perrone leather cleaner and conditioner
- Deep cleaning of galley including appliances, cabinetry, food surfaces, and waste
- Lavatory disinfecting including all surfaces, mirrors, and waste containment
- Cockpit cleaning using only approved materials (Sontara wipes, 50/50 IPA) on all
  sensitive instrumentation and avionics — controls are never moved or disturbed
- Window cleaning to a streak-free finish
- Application of Clean Linen air freshener (cabin only)
- Final FOD (Foreign Object Debris) check before signing off

**Materials:** Perrone leather cleaner/conditioner, 50/50 isopropyl alcohol, Sanicide EX3,
Sontara wipes for screens, Nuvite Extractor Fluid for carpet pre-spray, Magic Erasers.

## Exterior Detail

**What it is:** A complete exterior cleaning of all aircraft surfaces including fuselage,
wings, belly, engine nacelles/pylons, landing gear and wheel wells, cockpit windows, and
all exterior glass — leaving every surface streak-free, debris-free, and protected with
a spray wax finish.

**Why it matters:** Aircraft exteriors accumulate exhaust carbon, hydraulic fluid, bug
debris, road grime, and atmospheric contaminants with every flight. Left uncleaned, these
accelerate paint degradation, increase drag, and allow corrosive materials to remain in
contact with paint and metal surfaces. A clean aircraft also makes inspection of the
exterior condition significantly easier.

**What's included:**
- Full degreasing of wheel wells, landing gear, belly, nacelles, and flap wells using
  Carbon X and Citricut Xtra
- Fuselage, crown, and tail cleaned and rinsed
- Application of Wash Wax All spray wax to painted surfaces for protection and shine
- NuPower applied to heavily degraded or oxidized paint areas
- All exterior windows and cockpit windscreen cleaned to streak-free finish with
  approved alcohol-based cleaner
- Dry wash pole technique used on upper surfaces to avoid damage to antennas and trim

## Carpet Extraction

**What it is:** Deep hot-water extraction of all aircraft carpeting using professional
extraction equipment (AquaPro Vac), combined with pre-spray treatment to break down
embedded contaminants, odors, and staining that vacuuming alone cannot remove.

**Why it matters:** Aircraft carpets are subject to heavy foot traffic, tracked-in
contaminants, food and beverage spills, pet dander, and fine particulate matter that
embeds deep into carpet fibers over time. Regular vacuuming addresses surface debris
but does not remove what has worked into the pile. Extraction uses hot water and chemical
pre-treatment to flush out deep contamination, restoring the carpet's appearance,
eliminating odors, and preserving the carpet's structural integrity.

**We recommend carpet extraction for all aircraft at every maintenance event at Duncan**
regardless of apparent condition — because what isn't visible is often what matters most.

**Process:**
- Pre-spray with Nuvite Extractor Fluid to loosen embedded soil
- Hot water extraction pass using AquaPro Vac
- TuffStuff aerosol for grease spots as needed
- Aircraft left open to dry following extraction
